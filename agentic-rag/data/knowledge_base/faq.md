# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## Leave Related Questions

### Q: How do I check my remaining leave balance?
**A:** You can check your leave balance in three ways:
1. Ask the HR Assistant chatbot: "What is my leave balance?"
2. Check your monthly payslip
3. Log in to the HR portal at https://hr.techcorp.com
4. Email HR at hr@techcorp.com

### Q: Can I take leave during my probation period?
**A:** Yes, but with limitations:
- Sick leave: Available from day one
- Casual leave: Available after completing 1 month
- Earned leave: You start accruing it from day one but can only use it after completing your probation period (3 months)

### Q: What happens to my unused leave at the end of the year?
**A:**
- **Casual Leave**: Up to 5 days carry forward; remaining days lapse
- **Sick Leave**: Up to 10 days carry forward (max accumulation: 20 days)
- **Earned Leave**: Unlimited carry forward

### Q: How much notice do I need to give for taking leave?
**A:**
- Casual Leave: Minimum 2 days advance notice
- Sick Leave: As soon as possible (same day acceptable for genuine illness)
- Earned Leave: 15 days advance notice for leaves longer than 3 days
- Emergency Leave: Inform immediately, submit formal application within 24 hours

### Q: Can I take leave for half a day?
**A:** Yes, half-day leave is allowed. It will deduct 0.5 days from your leave balance. Please provide at least 1 day advance notice to your supervisor.

## Working Hours Questions

### Q: What are the standard working hours?
**A:** Standard working hours are 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday to Friday, with a 1-hour lunch break. This makes it 8 hours of work plus 1 hour break = 9 hours at office.

### Q: Is there flexibility in working hours?
**A:** Yes, we have flexible working hours with these rules:
- Core hours (10:00 AM to 4:00 PM) are mandatory
- You can start between 8:30 AM to 10:00 AM
- Corresponding end time adjustment required
- Manager approval needed for flex arrangements

### Q: Can I work from home?
**A:** Yes, after completing 3 months of employment:
- Maximum 2 days per week normally
- Requires department head approval
- Based on role suitability and performance
- Full availability during core hours required

### Q: What if I'm running late to office?
**A:** 
- Inform your supervisor immediately
- If using flex hours (before 10:00 AM), it's acceptable
- After 10:00 AM is considered late without prior flex approval
- More than 3 late arrivals per month may result in a warning

## Holiday Questions

### Q: How many public holidays do we get?
**A:** The company observes all official public holidays as declared by the government. The list is published at the beginning of each year. These are separate from your personal leave entitlements.

### Q: Do I need to work on weekends?
**A:** Generally, no. Saturday and Sunday are off. Weekend work requires prior approval and is compensated with either double pay OR compensatory off.

## Application Process Questions

### Q: How do I apply for leave?
**A:** Follow these steps:
1. Use the HR portal or tell the HR Assistant: "I want to apply for leave from [start date] to [end date]"
2. Specify leave type (casual, sick, or earned)
3. Provide reason for leave
4. Your supervisor will review within 24 hours
5. You'll receive email confirmation once approved

### Q: Can I cancel my approved leave?
**A:** Yes, you can cancel approved leave:
- Notify your supervisor immediately
- Update in the HR portal
- Your leave balance will be restored
- At least 24 hours notice required (unless emergency)

### Q: What if my leave application is rejected?
**A:** If rejected:
1. Check the reason provided by your supervisor
2. Discuss with your supervisor for alternative dates
3. If urgent, escalate to HR department
4. Emergency situations will be handled case-by-case

## Compensation and Benefits

### Q: Will I be paid for unused casual leave?
**A:** No, casual leave cannot be encashed. Use it within the year or carry forward up to 5 days.

### Q: Can I encash my earned leave?
**A:** Earned leave encashment is available subject to company policy, typically during exit or as per annual company announcements.

### Q: What if I fall sick during a public holiday?
**A:** Public holidays are separate from sick leave. You don't need to apply for sick leave for public holidays. If sick before/after a public holiday, normal sick leave rules apply.

## New Employee Questions

### Q: When will my leaves be credited?
**A:**
- **Casual Leave**: Full annual quota on joining (prorated if joining mid-year)
- **Sick Leave**: Full 10 days available from day one
- **Earned Leave**: Accrues monthly (1 day per month)

### Q: What documents do I need for leave application?
**A:** Generally, no documents needed for casual/earned leave. For sick leave:
- No document required for 1-3 days
- Medical certificate required for more than 3 consecutive days
- Hospital documents for hospitalization

### Q: Who approves my leave?
**A:**
- **Casual and Sick Leave (1-5 days)**: Immediate supervisor
- **Earned Leave (more than 5 days)**: Department head
- **Extended leave (more than 15 days)**: Requires HR approval

## Emergency Situations

### Q: What if I have a family emergency?
**A:** 
1. Inform your supervisor immediately by phone/email
2. Take the necessary time off
3. Submit formal leave application within 24 hours of returning
4. Provide supporting documentation if required
5. HR will handle on a case-by-case basis with empathy

### Q: Can I take unpaid leave?
**A:** Yes, unpaid leave is available if you've exhausted all paid leave:
- Requires approval from department head and HR
- Maximum duration typically 30 days per year
- Job security maintained during approved unpaid leave
